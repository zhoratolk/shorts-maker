<objective>
Rework the render layer to actually render the 6 transition types. Two pieces: (1) `build_transition_filter`, a pure enum-validated helper that returns the ffmpeg xfade-based fragment for a given transition type (following the existing `build_video_effects_chain`/`build_punch_zoom_filter` shape); and (2) restructure `build_jumpcut_command`'s flat N-ary `concat` into a hybrid that keeps today's exact flat-concat graph when no fancy transition is requested and switches to a sequential fold (concat for cut boundaries, xfade+acrossfade for non-cut boundaries, borrowing overlap from the pause gap) when at least one boundary asks for a transition. Then wire `render_clip` to read the per-boundary transition list from the plan entry and thread it through.

Purpose: TRANS-02 (render all 6 types) and TRANS-03 (insufficient-overlap boundary falls back to cut at the render layer too). render.py stays a pure executor — it consumes a pre-computed `boundary_transitions` list from the plan entry (produced by 04-04's analysis, injected by 04-06's orchestration); it never analyzes content itself, honoring the project's "render.py never reasons about content" constraint. The transition-selection and filter-building functions are written generically so Phase 5's cross-clip stitching can reuse them.
Output: reworked `scripts/render.py` + `tests/test_render.py` unit coverage, with every pre-existing render test still green.
</objective>
